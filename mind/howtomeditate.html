MBM Guide to Meditation

Remember, meditation is for enjoyment and should not feel like a chore. While it may not immediately solve life's challenges, it can provide a clearer mind and a calmer disposition to better tackle them. Embrace meditation as a journey of self-discovery and relaxation, not as a quick fix. With consistent practice, you'll find it to be a valuable part of your daily routine.

Guide to Meditation

Contrary to the popularization of meditation you do not need to be guided. Guided meditation is certainly an excellent place to start however it is not necessary. After building a comfortability with practicing meditation we recommend you end your sessions with guided meditation and continue on your own.

Essentials for Meditation:

  • Environment: A clean setting is preferred, but not essential. Since your eyes will be closed during meditation, your surroundings may vary.
  • Minimize Disturbances: Itâ€™s especially important for beginners to ensure a quiet space. Turn off phone notifications, separate from others who may distract you (including pets), and try to find a place with as little noise as possible. Perhaps enjoy being outside weather permitting.
  • Use a Timer: Crucial for developing self-discipline. Initially, a timer helps maintain focus. Over time, as your practice deepens, the perception of time may change, making the timer less necessary.
  • Comfort: Choose a comfortable position. Whether lying, sitting, or standing, comfort is key to maintaining focus and avoiding sleep.
  • Address Physical Needs: Ensure you're not hungry, thirsty, or otherwise physically uncomfortable before starting.

Meditation Steps:

  1. Eyes: Begin by closing your eyes to reduce visual distractions. Although possible, meditating with open eyes is not recommended for beginners.
  2. Comfort: Relax your muscles and breathe deeply. Try the 8-4-8 breathing technique (modified 4-7-8 technique) by inhaling for 8 seconds through your nose, hold for 4, and exhaling for 8 through your mouth to calm your mind.
  3. Awareness: Aim for a state of pure awareness. Focus on simple sensations like your breath, heartbeat, or subtle sounds to clear your mind.
  4. Thoughts: Don't struggle against your thoughts. Acknowledge them and return to your focus. Trying too hard may cause anxiety.
  5. Duration: Test yourself to find a comfortable meditation length. Gradually increase your time as you become more comfortable.
  6. Movement: Try to remain still, but if absolutely necessary, adjust your position to maintain comfort.
  7. The Quiet: With practice, you'll start to 'hear' the silence, a profound feeling of stillness and disconnection from the external world.
  8. Refreshed: Open your eyes. After meditation, you'll likely feel rejuvenated, often leading to personal insights and motivations.

Aim to meditate at least 5 minutes a day and quickly work your way to 10 minutes. Over time you should increase your practice till you can confidently meditate a total of 20 or more minutes a day. This time be broken into one or two sessions per day.
