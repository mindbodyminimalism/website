Our Mission, Our Story, Our Philosophy

Our mission is to bring all the tools necessary to create a good, meaningful, and healthy life into one accessible and comprehensible hub. Through the use of logic, philosophy, and scientific research Mind Body Minimalism distributes curated information and incorprates our findings into simple and intuitive tools. We are dedicated to empowering individuals to navigate the complexities of life with greater ease and clarity.

Founder and Creator Shane Draper has spent the last ten years to exploring the essence of a fulfilling life. The idea of a "good" life is a complex and multifaceted subject within philosophy, encompassing a range of traditions, cultures, and individual viewpoints. Each perspective offers a unique take on what constitutes a meaningful life, illustrating the vast array of human belief and cultural norms. A concept deemed as contributing to a "good" life in one philosophical doctrine may be entirely different in another. It is also common for individuals to integrate elements from various philosophical teachings to shape their personal definition of a good life.

Despite the wide array of views, several universal themes consistently emerge across different philosophies regarding a "good" life. Attaining such a life typically involves introspection, purposeful action, learning, and participating in one's community. It demands continuous efforts to align one's deeds with personal values, cultivate supportive relationships, seek personal growth in various dimensions, and contribute positively to society.

Mind Body Minimalism adopts a methodical approach similar to the scientific method, grounded in evidence, logic, and reasoning, to uncover truths while also considering significant philosophical questions to resolve ethical and moral challenges. While science is empirical, focusing on patterns and probabilities, it often arrives at conclusions that echo longstanding philosophical views on the essence of a good life. The key difference lies in the approach and validation techniques: science is empirical and seeks reproducibility, whereas philosophy often relies on logic, ethics, and self-examination. Nevertheless, both fields provide essential insights into the elements of a content and healthy life.

In the digital age, one might expect accessing knowledge for leading a good life to be simpler. However, the internet, with its vast array of websites, articles, and multimedia content, often leads to more confusion than clarity. The widespread presence of misleading and conflicting information—ranging from anecdotal evidence and biased studies to outright falsehoods—impedes our quest for self-improvement. Sifting through this information to find reliable sources can be overwhelming.

Driven by years of learning and meta-analysis, Shane established Mind Body Minimalism to offer a trusted space for accurate life-enhancing information. The platform aims to simplify the application of this knowledge, enabling users to embark on their self-improvement journey, even if they are just starting to grasp the underlying principles. As the body of knowledge and research evolves, so will the website and its resources, ensuring they continue to meet the community's needs effectively.
